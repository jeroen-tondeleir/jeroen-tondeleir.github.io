IT autobiography

December 15, 2015

IT and me?

Hi, my name is Jeroen Tondeleir and I am currently 18 years old, I’m in 6I at the Immaculata Institute.

I got in touch with IT at school, when we needed to work on computers.
When I was 6 years old I received my first computer from my parents, it was a pc from Olivetti, and I was very happy with it although I couldn’t do much with it. I was quite amazed with the things I could do with it, especially the games opened a new world for me because I couldn’t access the internet back then. The games I could play were Minesweeper, 3D pinball and some card game.

Through the years IT got a new meaning for me, I began to discover that I could do more with a pc than play games. Since I knew that, my knowledge has kept on growing and I have started working with Microsoft’s products and some other programs. I find IT a very interesting and handy, but dangerous subject. I think it’s dangerous because it helps us so much in our daily life that we forget how to do things without it. For example a lot of people use a gps almost daily, if it suddenly doesn’t work anymore people will have a much harder time finding out the way to get to a location because they have worked more with a gps than with maps.

At this moment IT means a lot to me, because it allows me to work more efficiently and faster, it also helps me to get better grades because my handwriting is almost unreadable. I normally spend at least 4 hours each day on my computer. I work on a netbook, it’s very small but easy to take with you. I’m fascinated with the fact that IT opens a new world: instead of using books and looking information up, that would probably take ages, you can just look it up on the internet, there are a lot of handy tools you can use for free. There is definitely an evolution if I look back how I evolved because of IT, but it’s too much to sum up right now. If someone asked me if I was an expert, I would have to answer that negatively. I say this because I’m definitely not an expert at all, I know more than the people who don’t follow IT at school, but I still have a lot to learn.

This year I’m doing two internships at two different companies, the reason for this was because we thought it could help me to make a decision about my future and to learn more about hardware and software. After a month I realized that software was no good for me, but I happily discovered that I was pretty good when stuff came down to hardware. So I think if I decided to continue or fall back to IT it would probably be in the hardware sector.

Right now my vision of the future has changed quite a lot, at the end of the previous year I decided that I currently didn’t want to continue in IT. Currently I have set my eyes on Integrated Security, that topic seems a lot more fun than IT to me. The fact why I chose to do this is probably because IT doesn’t interest me that much anymore. However my current choice does not exclude IT from later in my life, but right now I just don’t feel like it anymore.

Integrated Security is not like Safety First, but it has more interesting concepts about it. I would love to learn more about safety prevention and another thing that’s very nice is the fact that you can get a job very quickly after you graduate. All these little things have contributed to my decision..

When I think about my aspirations, I just can’t think of any actually.

My life is quite intertwined with my pc, I am using it too much and not only to work for school, I used it too much to relax myself: like movies, browsing YouTube etc. IT has become a huge factor in my life because it will probably influence my life, hobbies and job later, it also is one of my biggest interests.
